How Value Flows Across Chains & Card Rails

From wallets and exchanges to processors, networks and issuers – how “chain payments” actually move.

Compare Crypto & Digital-Friendly Cards

What is a “chain payment” in card terms?

A chain payment is any flow where the value starts in one system (for example a blockchain, wallet, exchange or alternative rail) and ends up as a standard card transaction on Visa/Mastercard rails.

  • Crypto and stablecoin wallets that issue cards
  • Exchanges that offer “spend your balance” cards
  • Fintechs that bridge bank accounts, wallets and cards
  • Token-based loyalty that settles through card rails

End-to-end chain → card flow

1. Value on chain

You hold BTC, ETH, stablecoins or tokenized balance in a wallet, exchange or app.

2. Conversion or pre-funding

Provider converts assets to fiat instantly at spend time, or pre-loads a fiat balance behind the card.

3. Card rails

The transaction runs on the usual four-party model: terminal → acquirer → network → issuer.

4. Settlement & fees

Merchant receives local currency; your on-chain or wallet balance is reduced, including spread and fees.

Who sits where in the payment chain?

Actor Role in chain Typical risks
Wallet / exchange Holds your assets, triggers conversion and issues or powers the card. Custody, hacks, regulatory changes, solvency.
Programme manager / issuer Issues the card, sets limits, handles disputes and KYC. Credit risk, compliance, chargebacks.
Processor / acquirer Connects merchants and terminals to the card networks. Tech outages, routing choices, fee structure.
Network (Visa / Mastercard) Routes authorisations and clearing between bank sides. Global rules, scheme fees, brand rules.
Merchant & terminal Accepts the payment; sees only fiat transaction data. Terminal configuration, acceptance, local restrictions.

Why the chain matters for users

Understanding the full chain helps you choose better products and avoid surprises:

  • Fees and FX spreads may sit at the wallet, exchange, issuer or network level
  • Declines can come from risk engines at multiple hops in the chain
  • Chargeback and refund rules follow the card issuer – not the blockchain
  • Regional restrictions can block specific rails even if others work
  • Reward tokens or cashback may live on-chain while spend happens off-chain

Common ChainPay design patterns

Auto-convert at spend

Assets stay in crypto until the moment you tap; provider sells in real time per transaction.

Pre-loaded fiat behind a card

You convert manually into a fiat balance; card spends from that balance like a normal prepaid card.

Stablecoin treasury + card

Company or DAO holds stablecoins; card programme uses them as funding source for global spend.

On-chain loyalty + off-chain spend

Traditional card for payments, with rewards issued as tokens or on-chain points.
